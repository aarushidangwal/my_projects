Steven Paul Jobs

1955 – 2011

Techno Man

Steven Paul Jobs was born on February 24, 1955 in San Francisco, California. His unwed biological parents, Joanne Schieble and Abdulfattah Jandali, put him up for adoption. Steve was adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs, a lower-middle-class couple, who moved to the suburban city of Mountain View a couple of years later. The Santa Clara county, south of the Bay Area, became known as Silicon Valley in the early 1950s after the sprouting of myriads of semi-conductor companies in the area. As a result, young Steve Jobs grew up in a neighborhood filled with engineers working on electronics and other gizmos in their garages on weekends. This shaped his interest in the field as he grew up. At age 13, he met one the most important persons in his life: 18-year-old Stephen Wozniak, an electronics whiz-kid —and an incorrigible prankster, much like Steve himself.

Life Lessons from Steve Jobs

His life and teachings, through documented videos like interviews and lectures, offer insights into his way of thinking or approach. These provide nuggets for aspiring individuals in any field, as Jobs is not all about technology but an interpreter of distinct perspectives. Let’s look at some of them.

  • His famous saying, “The only way to do great work is to love what you do,” clearly reflects following passion.
  • With the slogan “Think different,” he challenged the status quo and encouraged others to navigate the path of creativity.
  • He embraced failure and learned from it to his triumphant return from NeXt and Pixar to Apple in 1997.
  • He always believed “Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication,” which made him work intuitively towards user-friendly products.
  • He insisted on perfection with attention to detail to meet the standards of both quality and aesthetics.
